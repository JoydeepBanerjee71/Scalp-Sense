Your probable reasons for hairloss

Dieting

Extreme dieting or inadequate nutrition can deprive the body of essential nutrients required for healthy hair growth, leading to hair loss.

Flaking

Flaking of scalp due to conditions like dandruff or seborrheic dermatitis can weaken hair follicles and lead to hair loss.

Stress

Chronic stress can disrupt the hair growth cycle leading to increased shedding and eventual hair loss.

Iron Deficiency

Iron deficiency can lead to anemia which can result in poor circulation to the scalp and insufficient oxygen and nutrient delivery to hair follicles, causing hair loss.

Rash

Scalp conditions like psoriasis or eczema can cause inflammation and irritation leading to hair loss in affected areas.

Heredity

Genetic predisposition, particularly in men, can cause a condition called male pattern baldness, which leads to progressive hair thinning and eventual baldness.

FAQ's

There are several ways to classify baldness stages :

  • Normal : No significant hair loss or receding hairline.

  • Stage 1: Very slight recession of the hairline at the temples.

  • Stage 2: Noticeable receding hairline, forming a higher forehead.

  • Stage 3: Significant recession of the hairline, often forming an "M" or "U" shape when viewed from above. Hair loss might also begin on the crown.

  • Bald : Extensive hair loss with a horseshoe-shaped pattern remaining on the sides and back of the head.

Treatment options depend on the stage of baldness and individual factors. Here are some possibilities:

  1. Medications: Minoxidil and finasteride are FDA-approved medications for male pattern baldness.

  2. Hair transplant: This surgical procedure involves transplanting hair follicles from a donor area to the balding area.

  3. Scalp micropigmentation: This cosmetic procedure creates the appearance of a shaved head by tattooing tiny dots on the scalp.

  4. Hairpieces or wigs: These can provide a temporary or permanent solution for covering bald areas.

Hair loss is a common concern for men, and it doesn't necessarily affect your health. However, if you're experiencing rapid hair loss or it's causing you emotional distress, it's wise to consult a doctor or dermatologist to discuss treatment options and address any underlying concerns.
